PHILIPPINE EAGLE
Philippine eagle (Pithecophaga jefferyi) also known as the monkey-eating eagle or great Philippine eagle, is a critically endangered species of eagle of the family Accipitridae which is endemic to forests in the Philippines. It has brown and white-colored plumage, a shaggy crest, and generally measures 86 to 102 cm (2.82 to 3.35 ft) in length and weighs 4.04 to 8.0 kg (8.9 to 17.6 lb).

TAMARAW
Tamaraws (Bulbalus mindorensis) also known as mindoro dwarf buffalos. They are herbivores and mostly consume grasses, bamboo shoots and wild sugar cane. Tamaraws have been subjected to hunting for food and entertainment purposes in the past, which is why they are critically endangered.

TARSIER
Tarsiers (Tarsiidae) are small nocturnal primates that follow a carnivore diet. They mostly feed on various types of insects. Tarsiers tend to shy away from human contact, and are very sensitive to bright lights, physical contact and noises.

PHILIPPINE CROCODILE
Philippine crocodile (Crocodylus mindorensis) is a kind of freshwater crocodile that is relatively small. Hatchlings are white on their ventral side and have a golden-brown dorsum with transverse dark stripes. The brown ages and gets darker. They have a comparatively large snout and thick dorsal armor compared to other crocodiles.

BLEEDING HEART PIGEON
Bleeding heart pigeon (Gallicolumba luzonica) refers to a dark, crimson patch of feathers on its breast that resembles a wound that is bleeding. Males attract females during breeding season by displaying an enlarged breast to showcase their brilliant blood marking, or "heart."

CALAMIAN DEER
Calamian deer (Axis calamianensis) has short legs, short stocky legs , and slightly raised hindquarters. The underparts are paler than the rest of the body. Dark brown (nearly black in some species) characterizes the legs, and a dark dorsal stripe runs from the shoulders to the bushy tail's base. White lips with dark bars encircling them. There is a little, whitish "bib" on the upper throat.

SULU HORNBILL
Sulu hornbill (Anthracoceros montani) is a type of hornbill that belongs to the Bucerotidae family. It is an endangered species thought to have been hunted to extinction on Jolo. Tropical damp forests are its natural habitat.

PALAWAN FRUIT BAT
Palawan Fruit bat (Acerodon leucotis) are herbivores and mostly consume leaves, fruits, flowers, sap, and twigs. Like most bats they are nocturnal.

TAWI-TAWI DOVE
Tawitawi brown dove (Phapitreron cinereiceps) is an endangered species of bird in the Columbidae family distinguished by its orange-peach breast. It most likely has a very small population and has had a very quick population drop due to a reduction in the size of its forest habitat, which is currently only found in a few small, remnant portions spread across a very short area. As a result, it is listed as endangered.
