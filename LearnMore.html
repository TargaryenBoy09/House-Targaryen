Philippine Endangered Species

In the Philippines, only four percent of the native forest habitat of certain unique species remains. Unsustainable fishing at sea has diminished fish populations, putting pressure on the ocean's capacity to produce food and revenue.

The country is also a center for illegal wildlife trafficking on a global scale, and the local trade in exotic pets and bush meat continues to harm biodiversity. Authorities are largely overburdened by the enormous number of illicit activity and lack the resources to enforce national protection legislation.
